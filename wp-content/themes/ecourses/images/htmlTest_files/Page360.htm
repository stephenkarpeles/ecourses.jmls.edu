Library Newsletter for Faculty, 2012

Those of you who are familiar with Bloomberg’s financial and news services will be interested to know that Bloomberg recently purchased BNA and will be integrating BNA content and services into the Bloomberg  platform. Bloomberg Law integrates comprehensive legal content, company and market information, and proprietary news all in one place.  Bloomberg offers court opinions, its own citator, docket information via PACER, and secondary sources from Practicing Law Institute as well as BNA. It also provides sample corporate documents and a user-friendly search interface for public company SEC filings. One nice feature of Bloomberg for law schools is that the docket search and retrieval feature via PACER is free, at least for now.

In an effort to broaden its exposure, Bloomberg Law is free to law school faculty and students who choose to register. If you would like a username and password to access Bloomberg, or if you would like a demonstration for yourself or one of your classes, please contact Tom Keefe (8keefe@jmls.edu) in the Library.

Course in Digital Law Practice

The Center for Computer-Assisted Legal Instruction (CALI)  is offering a free online course called Topics in Digital Law Practice for students and law professors. This course is designed to provide an overview of the changes that are occurring in the practice of law today, especially with respect to technology. It will introduce law students to real-world situations that they will encounter in the job market and point law professors to new avenues to cover in their courses.

The course, which began on Feb. 10, will run for 9 weeks.  Each week a 30 minute lecture will be presented via webcast, followed by a Q&A session. If you missed the first week, don’t worry.  All the lectures are recorded and available online.  For more information and to register, visit

http://tdlp.classcaster.net/

Hein Online app for

iDevices

HeinOnline, has a new iPhone/iPad/itouch app to download. Researchers can read articles and documents in PDF, browse by volume, navigate a volume with the electronic table of contents, and use full advanced searching techniques. The app will allow access to all of the HeinOnline collections our library subscribes to including the Law Journal Library, the Intellectual Property Library, and the U.S. Federal Legislative History Library.

To make sure that the app continues to work for you, you will need to access the app at least every thirty days while on the JMLS wireless network. If you have questions about using this app, contact Raizel Liebler, Research Services Librarian, at 8liebler@jmls.edu.
