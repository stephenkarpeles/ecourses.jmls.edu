JMLS’ Updated Guide to Publishing in Law Reviews and Journals

This short guide (http://libraryguides.jmls.edu/publishinginlawreviews ) is intended to assist JMLS faculty who wish to submit articles for publication in law reviews and journals. The guide covers both deciding where to publish and law review submission services, and additional information such as Allen Rostron & Nancy Levit’s recently updated table of journal policies for publication, Information for Submitting Articles to Law Reviews & Journals and Professor Colin Miller’s  Submission Guide for Online Law Review Supplements.

Need additional assistance? Contact your library liaison or Raizel Liebler (8liebler@jmls.edu), Research Services Librarian.

 

Index to Legal Periodicals

The Index to Legal Periodicals, previously available through the WilsonWeb platform, is now available through EBSCOhost.  What does this mean for you?  You can now search for legal and non-legal articles in the same place!

The library subscribes to EBSCOhost for the popular Academic Search Complete, a comprehensive database featuring scholarly articles  from a wide range of disciplines, and Business Source Complete, a database of scholarly business and industry periodicals.   Now the Index to Legal Periodicals Full-Text and Index to Legal Periodicals Retrospective (1908-1981) are also available via EBSCOhost.  You can choose to search just Index to Legal Periodicals or you can combine databases and search for articles across multiple disciplines at once.

 

 

Scholars and  Students

 

The library is continuing its Scholars and Students series this spring. This series of informal lunchtime conversations between faculty and students is designed to provide students with an opportunity to learn about our faculty’s scholarship interests. This month’s featured speakers are Allen Kamp on Feb. 20 and Tim O’Neill on  Feb. 27.
